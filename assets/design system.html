04 / Visual Style

Glassmorphism

Rules and application of frosted glass effects. Essential for maintaining depth and legibility over complex laboratory backgrounds or dense data.

Primary Glass Panel

Used for main content cards floating above imagery or dense backgrounds.

bg-white/40 backdrop-blur-xl border border-white/60

Dark Glass Panel

Used for tooltips, modals, or focused data overlays.

bg-clinical-dark/60 backdrop-blur-xl border border-white/10

Glass Rules

  • Use Sparingly: Apply only to elevate primary content over busy backgrounds.
  • High Contrast: Ensure text contrast meets AAA standards even through the blur.
  • Borders: Always use a subtle border (e.g., white/60) to define the glass edge.
  • Don't Stack: Avoid stacking multiple glass layers, it degrades performance.
05 / Data Visualization

Charts & Dosage Data

Standards for displaying critical medical data. Example: Liquid medicine prescription dosage based on patient weight.

Visualization Rules

  • Brand Colors for Data: Use Brand Teal for safe/primary data, and Product Red for critical limits.
  • Grid Lines: Keep grid lines subtle (opacity 10-20%) to avoid cluttering the view.
  • Interactive Tooltips: Provide exact values on hover for precise dosage reading.
